Homily 5: On the Side of Christ

Moscow 385, 94 r°-v°: Severus, from a homily.

Since indeed the side of the first man formed, Adam, that is Eve1, became for us the origin of evil, having accepted the deception of the serpent; for this reason Emmanuel, who for us has been called the second Adam2, after His voluntary crucifixion, had His side pierced by the soldier's lance3, restoring the woman who had been formed from that place, transforming her from softness to manly courage and to struggles for piety and granting her a share of His own steadfastness, and from her giving us the first fruits of immortality. For after His expiration and death according to the flesh, by which He put death to death, He permitted His side to be wounded, and from it poured forth the miraculous stream of blood and water4 which secures for us incorruptibility, showing that from where death took its origin, from there immortality has blossomed.
