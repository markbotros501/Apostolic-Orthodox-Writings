Chapter Six

"But Job answered and said:"1 [Commentary text for verse 1]

"Oh that my grief were thoroughly weighed, and my calamity laid in the balances together!"2 [Commentary text for verse 2]

"For now it would be heavier than the sand of the sea: therefore my words are swallowed up."3 [Commentary text for verse 3]

"For the arrows of the Almighty are within me, the poison whereof drinketh up my spirit: the terrors of God do set themselves in array against me."4 [Commentary text for verse 4]

"Doth the wild ass bray when he hath grass? or loweth the ox over his fodder?"5 [Commentary text for verse 5]

"Can that which is unsavoury be eaten without salt? or is there any taste in the white of an egg?"6 [Commentary text for verse 6]

"The things that my soul refused to touch are as my sorrowful meat."7 [Commentary text for verse 7]

"Oh that I might have my request; and that God would grant me the thing that I long for!"8 [Commentary text for verse 8]

"Even that it would please God to destroy me; that he would let loose his hand, and cut me off!"9 [Commentary text for verse 9]

"Then should I yet have comfort; yea, I would harden myself in sorrow: let him not spare; for I have not concealed the words of the Holy One."10 [Commentary text for verse 10]

"What is my strength, that I should hope? and what is mine end, that I should prolong my life?"11 [Commentary text for verse 11]

"Is my strength the strength of stones? or is my flesh of brass?"12 [Commentary text for verse 12]

"Is not my help in me? and is wisdom driven quite from me?"13 [Commentary text for verse 13]

"To him that is afflicted pity should be shewed from his friend; but he forsaketh the fear of the Almighty."14 [Commentary text for verse 14]

"My brethren have dealt deceitfully as a brook, and as the stream of brooks they pass away;"15 [Commentary text for verse 15]

"Which are blackish by reason of the ice, and wherein the snow is hid:"16 [Commentary text for verse 16]

"What time they wax warm, they vanish: when it is hot, they are consumed out of their place."17 [Commentary text for verse 17]

"The paths of their way are turned aside; they go to nothing, and perish."18 [Commentary text for verse 18]

"The troops of Tema looked, the companies of Sheba waited for them."19 [Commentary text for verse 19]

"They were confounded because they had hoped; they came thither, and were ashamed."20 [Commentary text for verse 20]

"For now ye are nothing; ye see my casting down, and are afraid."21 [Commentary text for verse 21]

"Did I say, Bring unto me? or, Give a reward for me of your substance?"22 [Commentary text for verse 22]

"Or, Deliver me from the enemy's hand? or, Redeem me from the hand of the mighty?"23 [Commentary text for verse 23]

"Teach me, and I will hold my tongue: and cause me to understand wherein I have erred."24 [Commentary text for verse 24]

"How forcible are right words! but what doth your arguing reprove?"25 [Commentary text for verse 25]

"Do ye imagine to reprove words, and the speeches of one that is desperate, which are as wind?"26 [Commentary text for verse 26]

"Yea, ye overwhelm the fatherless, and ye dig a pit for your friend."27 [Commentary text for verse 27]

"Now therefore be content, look upon me; for it is evident unto you if I lie."28 [Commentary text for verse 28]

"Return, I pray you, let it not be iniquity; yea, return again, my righteousness is in it."29 [Commentary text for verse 29]

"Is there iniquity in my tongue? cannot my taste discern perverse things?"30 [Commentary text for verse 30]
