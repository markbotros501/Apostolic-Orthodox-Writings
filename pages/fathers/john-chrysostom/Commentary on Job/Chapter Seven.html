Chapter Seven

"Is there not an appointed time to man upon earth? are not his days also like the days of an hireling?"1 [Commentary text for verse 1]

"As a servant earnestly desireth the shadow, and as an hireling looketh for the reward of his work:"2 [Commentary text for verse 2]

"So am I made to possess months of vanity, and wearisome nights are appointed to me."3 [Commentary text for verse 3]

"When I lie down, I say, When shall I arise, and the night be gone? and I am full of tossings to and fro unto the dawning of the day."4 [Commentary text for verse 4]

"My flesh is clothed with worms and clods of dust; my skin is broken, and become loathsome."5 [Commentary text for verse 5]

"My days are swifter than a weaver's shuttle, and are spent without hope."6 [Commentary text for verse 6]

"O remember that my life is wind: mine eye shall no more see good."7 [Commentary text for verse 7]

"The eye of him that hath seen me shall see me no more: thine eyes are upon me, and I am not."8 [Commentary text for verse 8]

"As the cloud is consumed and vanisheth away: so he that goeth down to the grave shall come up no more."9 [Commentary text for verse 9]

"He shall return no more to his house, neither shall his place know him any more."10 [Commentary text for verse 10]

"Therefore I will not refrain my mouth; I will speak in the anguish of my spirit; I will complain in the bitterness of my soul."11 [Commentary text for verse 11]

"Am I a sea, or a whale, that thou settest a watch over me?"12 [Commentary text for verse 12]

"When I say, My bed shall comfort me, my couch shall ease my complaint;"13 [Commentary text for verse 13]

"Then thou scarest me with dreams, and terrifiest me through visions:"14 [Commentary text for verse 14]

"So that my soul chooseth strangling, and death rather than my life."15 [Commentary text for verse 15]

"I loathe it; I would not live alway: let me alone; for my days are vanity."16 [Commentary text for verse 16]

"What is man, that thou shouldest magnify him? and that thou shouldest set thine heart upon him?"17 [Commentary text for verse 17]

"And that thou shouldest visit him every morning, and try him every moment?"18 [Commentary text for verse 18]

"How long wilt thou not depart from me, nor let me alone till I swallow down my spittle?"19 [Commentary text for verse 19]

"I have sinned; what shall I do unto thee, O thou preserver of men? why hast thou set me as a mark against thee, so that I am a burden to myself?"20 [Commentary text for verse 20]

"And why dost thou not pardon my transgression, and take away mine iniquity? for now shall I sleep in the dust; and thou shalt seek me in the morning, but I shall not be."21 [Commentary text for verse 21]
